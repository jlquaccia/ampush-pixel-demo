Tipping Hints: Maximize Your Lyft Revenue

Driving with Lyft has a ton of benefits — from helping out with health insurance to offering cell phone discounts and fuel rewards, even part-time Lyft drivers can take advantage of the bonus system. But as well as these sure benefits, what can you do to get the most of a driving experience? There’s one easy and time-honored way to bump your hourly rate: tips!


Check out our advice for filling up the tip jar and maintaining that 5-star rating while you’re at it.

  • Music Maestro

    Lyft rides always go faster with a great soundtrack. Knowing a couple of radio stations in different genres is a good way to start. Ask your rider what they like to listen to and switch to a station that suits their tastes — they’ll appreciate the little gesture and recognize your awesome service. If you’re able, you could also provide an AUX cable for your riders to hook their smartphones into the car’s speakers, so they can choose the tunes. A ride with entertainment equals a big tip bonus!

  • Watering Hole

    Small water bottles, a dish of gum, or even some candy can be a great small investment to rack up the tips. Having water on hand can be an absolute lifesaver for a thirsty passenger, whilst something to nibble on is a great way to keep any motion sickness at bay, so travel-sensitive passengers will really appreciate your thoughtfulness. Even just having these items present in the car shows you’re taking the whole Lyft thing seriously, and you care about your passengers’ comfort. Just make sure to avoid nuts — you don’t need an allergic rider and a trip to the ER on your hands!

  • Stay Sweet

    If your rider was prompt and waiting in their designated spot, make sure to thank them for their courtesy. When you meet them, if your rider is weighed down by groceries or has their hands full, a small effort to open their door, pop the trunk, or take something off their hands will go a long way. In the same vein, checking whether they’re happy with your air-con or heating, if they’d prefer windows up or down, or just asking if their ride is comfortable are all great ways to prove you’re a conscientious driver.

  • Read Your Rider

    Is your rider up for chit-chat or absorbed into their smartphone? You can tell whether they’re looking for a conversation partner in a few ways — single riders getting into the backseat is probably an indication of privacy, whilst jumping in the front shows they might want to hear about your day. Intense talk between two or more riders could mean they need space, or general conversation might welcome another voice. Reading what kind of rider you’ve got is an important way to make your passengers feel safe and happy, so they’ll be more likely to drop a few dollars your way.

  • Shine On

    Lots of riders will be looking to get to know you — let them! It helps to have a few cool stories on hand about where you’re from, why you became a driver, or why you like Lyft, and don’t be afraid to ask your passengers how they’re doing too. Showing off your personality will always engage a rider, make them feel more connected to you and more likely to give a healthy tip.
