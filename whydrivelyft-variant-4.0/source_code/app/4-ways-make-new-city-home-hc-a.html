4 Ways to Make a New City Your Home

If you’re moving to a new city, I’ve got a ‘one weird trick’ for you. There’s one thing you absolutely need to do to start feeling at home in a new place: explore! Don’t stare at the walls of your new apartment and expect new opportunities to start knocking. Lyft is a great way to get out and about and learn your new city. Oh, and did I mention make money doing it? Here are the four ways driving with Lyft can make your new city feel like home.

  • Drive Like a Local

    I’ve picked people up from offices, family homes, restaurants, the theatre, the waterfront… there’s nowhere I haven’t been! It’s incredible how quickly I got to know the city just by exploring. After a week of making money with Lyft, I felt comfortable with major junctions, the highway, and even the tiny side streets.

  • Eat Like a Local

    One thing I’ve learned is Googling can only go so far. The real gems - best take-out, strongest coffee, great local beer - can only be found through true hometown know-how. Try to ask everyone who gets in your car, any new work friends, or your new neighbors about where they pick up their coffee or order their pizza from, and you’re bound to find those true secret hot-spots.

  • Meet Like a Local

    Joining groups and local clubs is a great tactic to meet new people, but you shouldn’t have to define yourself with a single activity or group of friends. Maybe you love yoga, or hanging out at the bar on Friday, or volunteering with a local charity – but that doesn’t mean you have to be ‘the yoga guy’ now. I made a ton of different friends meeting other Lyft drivers (yeah, they throw us parties sometimes) and my passengers, many who were new to the city themselves.

  • Live Like a Local

    Driving with Lyft wasn’t only great to meet new folks or see the city — technically, the first perk is the money. With extra income from Lyft, I could work flexibly and snatch up a great apartment before anyone else saw it, avoid weekend crowds when I did a Home Depot run, and spend a little bit more on a couch to make that apartment feel homey.
