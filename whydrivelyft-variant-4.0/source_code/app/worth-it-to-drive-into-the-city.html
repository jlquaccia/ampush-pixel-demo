Driver Pro Tip: It’s Worth it to Drive into the City

I’ve been a Lyft driver for over a year now. I live in a suburb of Denver, and depending on traffic, it can easily take me 45 minutes to an hour to drive into the city. When I started driving, I had an obvious question: Am I better off driving close to home, or taking the extra time to commute into the city?


I spent some time driving in both the city and the suburbs, and found that the answer was pretty clear. Even though there are some benefits to suburban driving, there are a few big reasons why you’re better off commuting into the city.

  • 1. The Bonus

    This one is a no brainer. Lyft almost always has higher demand for new drivers in cities, rather than the less populated suburbs that surround them. To entice drivers, the sign-up bonus for new drivers is often significantly higher in major metro areas.

  • 2. More, but shorter rides

    When I first started driving in the suburbs, I noticed pretty quickly that many of my riders were going a fair distance compared to what I’d seen in the city. As a result, I was seeing some significantly higher fares for each ride. Between those long rides however, I found myself driving quite a bit further for my next pick-up, and that downtime cut into my hourly earnings. Even though rides are often shorter in the city, your next passenger is usually close by, which makes a huge difference to your bottom line at the end of the week.

    (Bonus - More cancellation fees!)
    More ride requests in the city also means more ride cancellations. Which usually mean a cancellation fee for the driver. In the city, with another rider right around the corner, this can feel like free cash! In the suburbs, where your next pick-up might be several miles away, the few extra bucks don’t quite make up for the hassle.

  • 3. Less wear and tear on your car

    While suburban driving can offer a relaxing break from the traffic and congestion of the city, it comes with a semi-hidden cost. The sprawl of the suburbs means you are often driving 15 minutes or more to pick up your next rider. I found I was putting about half as many miles on my car each day driving in the city. Which means not only do I waste less time between passengers, I’ve cut my fuel and maintenance costs significantly.
