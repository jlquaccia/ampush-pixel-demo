Derrick P.

Austin, TX
2013 Volkswagen CC

"The best week I had driving Lyft was during SXSW conference in Austin. I made $2000 in 5 days!"

  • What made you decide to drive for Lyft?

    I signed up for Lyft as a way to pay my bills so that I could explore other interests and hobbies. I was burnt out working a full time job and Lyft was a great way for me take a break.

  • What are your favorite parts of driving for Lyft?

    The flexibility of the schedule and the different types of people I have met. I can drive the morning and evening commutes during the week, and have the day to get things done I want to do, and then choose if I want to drive on the weekends or not. It’s up to me.

  • Are there any downsides?

    It can feel isolating at times to be driving. A lot of passengers are working in the car or having a conversation with their fellow passengers. On occasion, I have gotten passengers who were inconsiderate of my car and treated me poorly. It happens from time to time.

  • What are your favorite times of day to drive?

    The morning commute and Friday night crowds. I like the morning crowd because people are excited for the day and I enjoy having a conversation in the morning. I find the Friday night crowds entertaining because people are letting loose for the weekend, and tell me all sorts of interesting stories about themselves. I know a good story is about to start when the rider says, You are my Lyft driver so I am never going to see you again...

  • How much do you make driving for Lyft?

    The best week I had driving Lyft was during SXSW conference in Austin. I made $2000 in 5 days. I drove around 10 hours a day that week, and the city was in a constant surge all week which helped the prices.

  • Do you have any advice or tips for new drivers?

    I always made it a point to have a clean, fresh looking car on the outside and inside. Passengers appreciate a clean ride. Mints and smart phone chargers are great additions that don’t cost a lot and passengers like. I experimented with other items, and mints were the most cost effective and easy for passengers to put into their pockets if they didn’t want one right away.

    The phone chargers were crucial as passengers would usually get in the car frantic and say my phone is about to die, and I’m on an important call, do you have a charger?!?! Also, from a driver side, if their phone is on they are more likely to give you a tip and rating at the end of the ride.

    Also, don’t forget to stop and stretch every few hours!

  • Do you have any interesting or funny stories to share from your time driving with Lyft?

    I once had a passenger full of drunk Canadians who were on a quest to eat all the flavors of Oreos that were available in the U.S. (Apparently Canada only sells the original Oreos) They asked me where they could find all the flavors and I took them to several different groceries stores so that we can track down all the flavors! The total fare was pretty large and made for a fun experience!

    One time a couple was fighting in my back seat and the argument got pretty heated until the woman blurted out, “I JUST WANT YOU TO PROPOSE TO ME!” Then there was a few moments of silence and then they started kissing in the backseat. I put on some romantic music and they started laughing.

  • Overall, how do you feel about the Lyft driving experience?

    Driving for Lyft was an enjoyable experience. It was a great way to take some down time in my life and figure out what I wanted to do next, and not have to wake up everyday wondering how I was going to pay my bills. It allowed me the freedom and flexibility to try out new hobbies and explore other forms of work. Generally, I found Lyft riders to be more personable and friendly than other ride serving services. That made the experience better in general.
