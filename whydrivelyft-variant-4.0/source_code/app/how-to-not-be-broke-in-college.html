How to Not be Broke When You’re in College

  • College is a time of great opportunity, learning and growth for students across the country. For many, it is also marked by ramen noodle dinners, PB&J, and paper towels you “borrowed” from a campus restroom. With the ever-rising cost of tuition, getting by on a shoestring budget has become the norm for college students across the US.

  • But it doesn’t have to be.

  • The times are changing. Technology has changed the way we communicate, learn, travel, and entertain ourselves. It’s also changing the way we earn money, by creating new opportunities to earn cash without the rigid schedule of a regular job.

Ridesharing and the New Gig Economy

  • The ridesharing trend has exploded in recent years, and not just as a convenient way of getting from place to place. Millions of drivers worldwide have taken advantage of the benefits in the form of flexible hours and great earning opportunities. And when it comes to satisfaction and benefits, Lyft is the clear best choice for drivers, with 76% of Lyft drivers reporting that they are satisfied with their experience (compared to only 49% of Uber drivers) [1].

  • The great news for busy college students: You get to choose your own hours and be your own boss.

  • Getting started as a driver couldn’t be easier. Just be 21 or older with an iPhone or Android and an approved vehicle, and apply to drive now.

  • To top it off, for a limited time, Lyft is offering a $500 bonus to new drivers who complete 150 rides in their first 45 days with this link. And with reported average earnings of $17.50 an hour [2], you’ll be able to afford a mountain of ramen noodles - or maybe even upgrade to something with a little protein in it.

  • What are you waiting for? Stop scraping by, and start earning some spending money on your own terms.
