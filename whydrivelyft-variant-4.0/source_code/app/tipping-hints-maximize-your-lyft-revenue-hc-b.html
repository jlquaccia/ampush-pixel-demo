5 Hints to Make Better Tips Driving for Lyft

Driving with Lyft has a ton of benefits — from helping out with health insurance to offering cell phone discounts and fuel rewards, even part-time Lyft drivers can take advantage of the bonus system. But as well as these sure benefits, there’s one easy and time-honored way to bump your hourly rate: tips! Check out our top five ways to fill up the tip jar and maintain that 5-star rating while you’re at it.

  • Music Maestro

    Lyft rides always go faster with a great soundtrack. Learn the radio stations in your city, and ask your rider what they like to listen to — they’ll appreciate the little gesture and recognize your awesome service. If you’re able, you can even provide an AUX cable for your riders, to let them choose the tunes. A ride with entertainment equals a big tip bonus!

  • Watering Hole

    Small water bottles, a dish of gum, or even some candy can be a great small investment to rack up the tips. Having water on hand can be an absolute lifesaver for a thirsty passenger, whilst something to nibble on is a great way to keep any motion sickness at bay, so travel-sensitive passengers will really appreciate your thoughtfulness.

  • Stay Sweet

    If your rider was prompt and waiting in their designated spot, make sure to thank them for their courtesy. Small efforts like opening their door, popping the trunk, or helping with bags will go a long way. In the same vein, checking whether they’re happy with the temperature, if they’d prefer windows up or down, or just asking if they are comfortable are great ways to ensure a nice ride!

  • Read Your Rider

    Is your rider up for chit-chat or absorbed into their smartphone? Intense talk between two riders could mean they need space, while light, open conversation might welcome another voice. Reading what kind of riders you have is an important way to make your passengers feel safe, comfortable, and happy, so they’ll be more likely to drop a few dollars your way.

  • Shine On

    Lots of riders will be looking to get to know you — let them! It helps to have a few cool stories on hand about where you’re from, why you became a driver, or why you like Lyft, and don’t be afraid to ask your passengers how they’re doing too. Showing off your personality will always engage a rider, make them feel more connected to you and more likely to give a healthy tip.
