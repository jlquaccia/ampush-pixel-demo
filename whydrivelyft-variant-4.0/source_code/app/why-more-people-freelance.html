Why More People are Choosing Freelance Over Traditional Part-Time Jobs

  • We’re living in the freelance era. According to the American Freelancers Union, 53.7 million Americans counted themselves as freelancers this year, a proportion rapidly moving towards the 20% mark. By 2027, the American Freelancers Union (AFU) predicts that the majority of working Americans will be freelancers, with around 86.5 million people giving up the nine-to-five grind for a flexible lifestyle. [1]

  • The value of working for one’s self hardly needs justifying: the freedom to make your own schedule, earn more when you want to, and take more time for yourself when you need it. In comparison, with the federal minimum wage still sitting at $7.25 per hour, weekly paychecks for many part-time jobs can see almost negligible.

  • Self-employment does come with some concerns. Freelancers are generally on their own to deal with health insurance, sick time, taxes, and other forms of employment support.

  • But recently, some companies are helping people bridge those gaps.

  • Top ride-share service Lyft provides several benefits and perks to help drivers, and has a reputation for listening to their drivers, incorporating their feedback, and recognizing their integral value to a successful company.

  • Lyft drivers receive fuel and phone discounts, access to health insurance plans, retirement solutions, tax support, and even roadside assistance. Contrast this with a part-time employer who actually isn’t required to provide anything – in fact, many use the ‘safe harbor’ technique of keeping their employee's hours down to avoid offering benefits. [2]

  • As technology changes the way we work, travel, and communicate, companies like Lyft are helping to usher in an era of self-employment, and leading the way with great working conditions, earning opportunities, and benefits for their drivers.
