The 10 Best Cities to Drive for Lyft

If you’re living in one of these cities, driving for Lyft will bump your earning by a serious chunk of cash every week!

  • Phoenix, Arizona

    Throw together a huge student population (host to both the University of Phoenix and campus buildings of Arizona State), sunshine that regularly pushes temps above the 100 mark, and the second cheapest gas prices in the whole country, and you’ve got Phoenix! Lyft drivers are constantly in demand in this desert hotspot.

  • Atlanta, Georgia

    Hartsfield-Jackson International takes the top spot as the busiest airport in the US, receiving around 8 million visitors per month and rising. With the airport as one of the best places to get multiple rides per hour, plus a huge city population, Atlanta is a top place to rack up the rides!

  • Orlando, Florida

    Is there any phrase more terrible than ‘renting a car to go to Disney world’? Pricey contracts, long lines of vacationing tourists, precious hours of your vacation eaten up by something so basic. Lots of visitors to Disney World and Universal are opting to take a ride-share instead, saving themselves some money and keeping Lyft drivers in Orlando extra busy.

  • Chicago, Illinois

    Recent surveys have shown that Chicago is one of the worst cities to be a commuter, with busy public transit stretching the average commute times. More and more workers are piling into Lyfts to get to work on time and skip out on the ‘L’, so drivers are making great tips every morning and afternoon.

  • Columbus, Ohio

    With an enrollment of over 59,000 at Ohio State, all these college kids and their parents will need a Lyft! September, December, and May are especially busy times for drivers in Columbus, so you can even hop on it just for a weekend side-hustle. Hey, maybe you’re a student yourself and are looking for some extra cash — if you’re over 21 and have a car, Lyft is definitely the answer.

  • New Orleans, Louisiana

    You’re already so lucky if you live in the stunning, unique surrounding of the Big Easy, but ride-sharing is definitely adding some extra perks. The city’s transit system is known as being super cool but kind of useless: the streetcars don’t serve the suburbs or the airport. That’s where Lyft comes in!

  • New York, New York

    Unsurprisingly, several studies place New York at the top of Lyft’s earnings-per-hour tree, with requests for shorter rides near constant. Lyft is heavily used by tourists and locals alike, whether they want to avoid the subway or have the streets navigated on their behalf. If you’re a New Yorker with a car, why aren’t you signed up for Lyft?

  • San Francisco, California

    As the world’s center of innovation and start-up genius, San Fran is always an early adopter of the latest tech and modernization strategies. After such a smooth transition into the new normal, hundreds of thousands of San Franciscans are regular Lyft users, meaning drivers are in high demand and tips are plentiful.

  • Boston, Massachusetts

    The number of households without a means of transport is climbing, especially in Boston. Nearly 40% of families don’t own a car, relying instead on public transport and ride-share services. That means Boston has a huge, growing demand for Lyft drivers — cash in now!

  • Los Angeles, California

    With the birthplace of Lyft just down the highway in Santa Barbara, we couldn’t leave LA off this list. Known back in the day as Zimride, founders John Zimmer and Logan Green wanted an easy way to share rides around SB campus as well as in the busy city of LA, without the concern for unvetted drivers. Now, Lyft is a great way for drivers in LA or the surrounding area to earn side $$.
